
# BLACKOUT - Hackathon Brief Summary & Project Proposal

**Event:** GDGC UDA Hackathon 2026 (GRIDAKL)

## The Premise
At 3:47 am on Tuesday, a solar flare struck Earth, instantaneously corrupting every digital record globally. The physical infrastructure—lights, internet, roads, hospitals—remains entirely intact. However, the data that gives it meaning has vanished. 

Accounts, property titles, medical records, and access credentials no longer exist. Banks, courts, and institutions are frozen.

## The Imminent Crisis
London (population 9 million) is currently T+14 hours post-flare. The city is temporarily running on "momentum"—people going to work and operating out of pure habit. But within the next 28 hours, this momentum will fail as urgent questions arise: *Who owns what? Who has the right to decide? How do you prove who you are?*

## Our Focus: Problem 04
**Credentials without verification:** How can society verify the skills of critical workers like doctors and engineers when every record that confirmed it is gone?

## Our Solution: Peer-to-Peer Credit System
We are building a decentralized "web of trust" to verify qualifications for important sectors, ensuring safety and function.

### Core Mechanics
* **Vouching:** Professionals issue cryptographic "credits" to endorse the skills of their peers based on observed competence.
* **Weighted Trust:** Credits from highly trusted individuals within a specific sector carry more weight. 
* **Skill-Specific Ledgers:** Trust is compartmentalized by sector (e.g., Medical, Engineering) to prevent cross-domain authority abuse.

### Proposed Architecture
* **Local-First Storage:** Users maintain their own ledger of received credentials locally.
* **P2P Verification:** Utilizing short-range protocols for secure, physical "handshakes" to transfer credits during in-person interactions.
